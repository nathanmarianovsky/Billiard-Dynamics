Elliptical Table
For the case of an elliptical table we set:
$\Omega = \Big\{ (x,y) \in \mathbb{R}^2 \hspace{.1cm} \Big| \hspace{.1cm} \frac{x^2}{a^2} + \frac{y^2}{b^2} < 1 \Big\} \subset \mathbb{R}^2 \hspace{.3cm} \text{with} \hspace{.3cm} a,b \in \mathbb{R}$
to represent the interior of the ellipse and can parametrize the ellipse $\partial\Omega$ itself via:
$\mathbf{r}: [0,2\pi] \rightarrow \partial\Omega \hspace{.3cm} \text{given by} \hspace{.3cm} \mathbf{r}(\theta) = (a\cos(\theta),b\sin(\theta))$
In this setup we see that the necessary data to plot a trajectory in our system consists of $a$ (the horizontal axis of the ellipse), $b$ (the vertical axis of the ellipse), $\theta$ (starting point prescribed by an angle), $\mathbf{\dot{x}}(0)$ (initial velocity with two components), $B_1$ (strength of the inner magnetic field), $B_2$ (strength of the outer magnetic field), and the number of iterations.
