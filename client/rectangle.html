Rectangular Table
For the case of a rectangular table we set:
$\Omega = \Big\{ (x,y) \in \mathbb{R}^2 \hspace{.1cm} \Big| \hspace{.1cm} |x| < a \land |y| < b \Big\} \subset \mathbb{R}^2 \hspace{.3cm} \text{with} \hspace{.3cm} a,b \in \mathbb{R}$
to represent the interior of the rectangle. In this setup we see that the necessary data to plot a trajectory in our system consists of $a$ (the horizontal axis of the square), $b$ (the vertical axis of the square), $\mathbf{x}(0)$ (starting point), $\varphi$ (initial velocity prescribed by an angle), $B_1$ (strength of the inner magnetic field), $B_2$ (strength of the outer magnetic field), and the number of iterations.
