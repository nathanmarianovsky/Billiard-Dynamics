Annular Table
For the case of an annular table we set:
$\Omega_1 = \{ (x,y) \in \mathbb{R}^2 \hspace{.1cm} \Big| \hspace{.1cm} x^2 + y^2 < r_1^2 \} \subset \mathbb{R}^2 \hspace{.3cm} \text{and} \hspace{.3cm} \Omega_2 = \{ (x,y) \in \mathbb{R}^2 \hspace{.1cm} \Big| \hspace{.1cm} x^2 + y^2 < r_2^2 \} \subset \mathbb{R}^2$
with $r_1 < r_2$ where the region in between the concentric circles forms the annulus $\mathbb{A}$, or in other words $\partial\mathbb{A} = \Omega_2 \setminus (\Omega_1 \cup \partial\Omega_1)$. In order to plot the trajectory in such a scenario we parametrize the inner boundary $\partial\Omega_1$ via:
$\mathbf{r}: [0,2\pi] \rightarrow \partial\Omega_1 \hspace{.3cm} \text{given by} \hspace{.3cm} \mathbf{r}(\theta) = (r_1\cos(\theta),r_1\sin(\theta))$
In this setup we see that the necessary data to plot a trajectory in our system consists of $r_1$ (the inner radius of the annulus), $r_2$ (the outer radius of the annulus), $\theta$ (starting point prescribed by an angle on the inner boundary), $\varphi$ (initial velocity prescribed by an angle), $B_1$ (strength of the inner magnetic field), $B_2$ (strength of the middle magnetic field), $B_3$ (strength of the outer magnetic field), and the number of iterations.
