Introduction
This website serves to provide vivid visualizations tied to the dynamics of billiards associated to a convex domain $\Omega \subset \mathbb{R}^2$ in the plane. Formally the goal is to study the trajectories a particle (or a billiard ball) traverses with different initial conditions (the position and velocity) and varying strengths of the magnetic field(s) when the motion is restricted to the plane. By introducing the magnetic fields:
$\mathbf{B} = B_\Omega(\mathbf{x})\mathbf{\hat{k}} \hspace{.3cm} \text{where} \hspace{.3cm} B_\Omega(\mathbf{x}) = \begin{cases} B_1, & \mathbf{x} \in \Omega \\ B_2, & \mathbf{x} \in \mathbb{R}^2 \setminus \Omega \end{cases} \hspace{.3cm} \text{with} \hspace{.3cm} B_1, B_2 \in \mathbb{R}$
we can conclude under classical mechanics that with mass $m > 0$ and charge $q > 0$ (where the sign has already been incorporated) the particle must satisfy the following law of motion govered by the Lorentz force (with no electric field):
$\mathbf{\ddot{x}} = \begin{cases} \frac{qB_1}{m}\mathbb{J}\mathbf{\dot{x}}, & \mathbf{x} \in \Omega \\ \frac{qB_2}{m}\mathbb{J}\mathbf{\dot{x}}, & \mathbf{x} \in \mathbb{R}^2 \setminus \Omega \end{cases} \hspace{.3cm} \text{where} \hspace{.3cm} \mathbb{J} = \begin{pmatrix} 0 & -1 \\ 1 & 0 \end{pmatrix}$
