Introduction
This website serves to provide vivid visualizations tied to the dynamics of billiards associated to an ellipse in the plane. Formally the goal is to study the trajectories a particle (or a billiard ball) traverses with different initial conditions (the position and velocity) and varying strengths of the magnetic field(s). By setting:
$\Omega = \Big\{ (x,y) \in \mathbb{R}^2 \hspace{.1cm} \Big| \hspace{.1cm} \frac{x^2}{a^2} + \frac{y^2}{b^2} < 1 \Big\} \subset \mathbb{R}^2 \hspace{.3cm} \text{with} \hspace{.3cm} a,b \in \mathbb{R}$
to represent the interior of the ellipse and introducing the magnetic fields:
$\mathbf{B} = B_\Omega(\mathbf{x})\mathbf{\hat{k}} \hspace{.3cm} \text{where} \hspace{.3cm} B_\Omega(q) = \begin{cases} B_1, & \mathbf{x} \in \Omega \\ B_2, & \mathbf{x} \in \mathbb{R}^2 \setminus \Omega \end{cases} \hspace{.3cm} \text{with} \hspace{.3cm} B_1, B_2 \in \mathbb{R}$
we can conclude under classical mechanics that with mass $m > 0$ and charge $q > 0$ (where the sign has already been incorporated) the particle must satisfy the following law of motion govered by the Lorentz force (with no electric field):
$\mathbf{\ddot{x}} = \begin{cases} \frac{qB_1}{m}\mathbb{J}\mathbf{\dot{x}}, & \mathbf{x} \in \Omega \\ \frac{qB_2}{m}\mathbb{J}\mathbf{\dot{x}}, & \mathbf{x} \in \mathbb{R}^2 \setminus \Omega \end{cases} \hspace{.3cm} \text{where} \hspace{.3cm} \mathbb{J} = \begin{pmatrix} 0 & -1 \\ 1 & 0 \end{pmatrix}$
Now by parametrizing the ellipse $\partial\Omega$ via:
$\mathbf{r}: [0,2\pi] \rightarrow \partial\Omega \hspace{.3cm} \text{given by} \hspace{.3cm} \mathbf{r}(\theta) = (a\cos(\theta),b\sin(\theta))$
we see that the necessary data to plot a trajectory in our system consists of $a$ (the horizontal axis of the ellipse), $b$ (the vertical axis of the ellipse), $\theta$ (starting point prescribed by an angle), $\mathbf{\dot{x}}(0)$ (initial velocity with two components), $B_1$ (strength of the inner magnetic field), $B_2$ (strength of the outer magnetic field), and the number of iterations.
